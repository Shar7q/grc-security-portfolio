## Scenario Description
Regional University issues laptop computers to faculty members to support teaching, research, and administrative responsibilities. These devices are university property, connect to the campus network, and may store or access sensitive institutional data including student records, grade information, and proprietary research.

Because faculty use these laptops both on-campus and remotely, the university faces elevated risk from data breaches, malware, and unauthorized access. This Acceptable Use Policy establishes rules governing how issued laptops must be used, defines security controls required to protect university assets, and outlines consequences for non-compliance—ensuring faculty members, IT staff, and the institution share a clear, common understanding of their respective obligations.

## 1. Purpose
This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) establishes guidelines for the appropriate use of university-issued laptop computers assigned to faculty members. The policy exists to protect university data, preserve the integrity of institutional systems, and ensure that issued devices are used in a responsible, secure, and lawful manner.

By defining clear boundaries for acceptable and prohibited use, the university safeguards sensitive information—including student records and research data—and reduces the risk of security incidents that could harm students, staff, and the institution.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to **all faculty members** who have been issued a university laptop. It governs **all activities performed on or through that device**, whether the laptop is used on campus, at home, or at any other location.

This policy does **not** apply to personally owned devices, even if those devices are used to access university systems.

---

## 3. Acceptable Use
Faculty members may use university-issued laptops for the following purposes:

- Preparing and delivering course materials, lectures, and assessments
- Conducting academic research consistent with university policy
- Communicating with students, colleagues, and institutional contacts via university-approved email and collaboration tools
- Accessing university systems, portals, and licensed software
- Storing work-related files in university-approved storage solutions (e.g., university OneDrive or SharePoint)
- Attending virtual meetings and conferences related to academic or administrative duties
- Participating in professional development activities

---

## 4. Prohibited Activities
The following activities are strictly prohibited on university-issued laptops:

- Installing unauthorized software, applications, or browser extensions not approved by IT
- Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal, obscene, or otherwise inappropriate content
- Using the device for personal business, freelance work, or commercial activities unrelated to university duties
- Sharing login credentials or allowing unauthorized individuals to use the device
- Disabling, bypassing, or tampering with security software (antivirus, firewall, MDM agent)
- Storing sensitive personal data (e.g., Social Security numbers, financial data) outside of approved encrypted storage
- Connecting to untrusted public Wi‑Fi networks without an active VPN connection
- Using the device for cryptocurrency mining or other resource-intensive personal activities
- Attempting unauthorized access to other systems, networks, or accounts

---

## 5. Security Requirements
All faculty members must comply with the following security controls:

- [ ] **Full-Disk Encryption:** BitLocker (Windows) or FileVault (macOS) must remain enabled at all times.
- [ ] **Strong Authentication:** Faculty must use a strong password (minimum 12 characters) and enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all university accounts.
- [ ] **Automatic Screen Lock:** The device must lock automatically after no more than 5 minutes of inactivity.
- [ ] **OS & Software Updates:** All operating system and software updates must be applied within 72 hours of release or as directed by IT.
- [ ] **VPN Use:** When connecting to university resources from off-campus, the university-provided VPN must be used.
- [ ] **Antivirus/EDR:** University-managed endpoint protection must remain active and must not be disabled.
- [ ] **Approved Storage Only:** University data must be stored in university-approved cloud storage or on encrypted local drives—not on personal cloud accounts (e.g., personal Google Drive, Dropbox).
- [ ] **Physical Security:** Laptops must not be left unattended in unsecured public locations. Devices must be stored securely when not in use.

---

## 6. Responsibilities

### 6.1 Faculty Member (Device User)
- Use the device only for permitted purposes as outlined in Section 3.
- Comply with all security requirements in Section 5.
- Report loss, theft, or suspected compromise immediately to IT (see Section 7).
- Return the device in good working condition at the end of the assignment period.

### 6.2 IT Department
- Configure devices with required security settings prior to issuance.
- Maintain and update endpoint protection, MDM enrollment, and OS patches.
- Provide technical support and respond to security incidents.
- Conduct periodic audits to verify policy compliance.

### 6.3 Compliance Officer / Office of Information Security
- Review and update this policy annually or after significant security events.
- Investigate reported violations and coordinate with HR and legal as needed.
- Maintain records of issued devices and signed acknowledgment forms.

---

## 7. Reporting Requirements
Faculty members must report the following events **within 24 hours** of discovery:

- Loss or theft of the university laptop
- Suspected malware infection or unauthorized access
- Accidental disclosure of sensitive university data
- Any security incident or anomalous device behavior

**Report to:** IT Help Desk — [helpdesk@university.edu](mailto:helpdesk@university.edu) | Ext. 5500  
**Escalation:** Office of Information Security — [security@university.edu](mailto:security@university.edu)

Failure to report a known incident in a timely manner may result in additional disciplinary action.

## 8. Enforcement & Consequences
Violations of this policy will be handled in accordance with university disciplinary procedures. All violations will be documented. Repeat violations will result in escalated consequences.

| Severity | Example | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Minor | Forgetting to lock the screen | Verbal/written warning; mandatory security awareness training |
| Moderate | Installing unauthorized software | Written warning; IT remediation; temporary device suspension |
| Serious | Disabling security controls; sharing credentials | Device revocation; formal HR review; potential contract impact |
| Critical | Data breach; illegal activity | Immediate device seizure; HR and legal action; potential termination |

## Policy Justification (Security Control Focus)
This policy requires full-disk encryption (Section 5) on all university-issued faculty laptops. Faculty routinely store or access sensitive data—including student grades, personally identifiable information (PII), and unpublished research—both on campus and remotely.

If a laptop is lost or stolen without encryption, data stored on the drive may be accessible to anyone with physical possession of the device, even if operating system login controls are bypassed. Full-disk encryption ensures that, even if the hardware falls into the wrong hands, the data remains unreadable without the correct credentials.

Given that faculty frequently travel to conferences and work from public spaces, this control is one of the highest-impact, lowest-friction safeguards the university can enforce. Modern OS-native solutions like BitLocker and FileVault impose virtually no performance penalty and can be centrally managed by IT.
